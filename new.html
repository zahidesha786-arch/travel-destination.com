About Image

A renowned 13th-century Sufi saint of the Suhrawardiyya order. His real name was Sheikh Rukn-ud-Din Abul Fateh.

Location: Multan, Punjab, Pakistan — on the Qasim Bagh hill.
Built In: The tomb was built between 1320–1324 AD by Ghiyas-ud-Din Tughlaq, a ruler of the Delhi Sultanate.
Architectural Style: It features Islamic architecture with multicolored glazed tiles, brickwork, and a massive dome. It is one of the finest pre-Mughal monuments in South Asia.
Importance: A major Sufi shrine. Visited by thousands of devotees every year. Listed on UNESCO’s tentative list for World Heritage status.
